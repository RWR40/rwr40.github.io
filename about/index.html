About

Founder of RoadwayVR Course Hub, an open-learning platform for transportation planning, simulation, and smart cities. I hold a BSc/MSc in Industrial Engineering (Optimization) and a PhD plus Postdoctoral training in Transportation Engineering. My work connects AI, traffic simulation, and immersive 3D visualization to support safer and more efficient transportation systems.

Optimization and AI in Transportation

Data-driven safety and decision-support methods grounded in real-world sensing and applied modeling.

OPT-AI Safety Analytics Decision Support Field Data
  • Next-Generation Roadside Animal Detection (Highway Trans-Canada 1, British Columbia): Real-time risk estimation using a fuzzy rule-based model to deliver graded warnings and support V2I communication.
  • IoT Protection System for Police Officers (Highway 416, Ontario): Risk analysis combining distance, speeding, and sight distance to generate early collision-risk warnings.

Traffic Simulation and Smart Cities

Simulation workflows for evaluating planning alternatives, policy, and operations with clear performance measures.

SIM SUMO PTV Vissim Planning Applications
  • Automated Speed Enforcement Education (Ontario): AI-assisted analysis of public feedback to identify common driver errors and produce targeted safety education videos.
  • Freight Transportation Visualization and Evaluation Platform (Highway 401, Durham Region): Simulation-based evaluation and 3D visualization of truck-friendly lane strategies using Vissim outputs and Unity.

Virtual Reality and 3D Visualization

Interactive 3D and VR tools that make transportation systems easier to understand, test, and communicate.

VR-3D Unity Co-simulation Visualization
  • Sumo2Unity (Open Source): Co-simulation toolkit integrating SUMO and Unity, including a ready 3D environment, vehicles, signals, and simulator vehicle setup.
  • Vissim–Unity VR Human-in-the-Loop Driving Simulator (Highway 401, Durham Region): High-frequency trajectory exchange enabling VR driving experiments with steering wheel and pedals for controlled evaluation of corridor and lane strategies.
